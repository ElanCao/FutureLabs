# McKinsey Skill Change Index: Research Brief

**Date:** March 25, 2026
**Source:** McKinsey Global Institute, "Agents, robots, and us: Skill partnerships in the age of AI" (November 25, 2025)

---

## Executive Summary

McKinsey's November 2025 report introduces the **Skill Change Index (SCI)**—a metric measuring skill exposure to automation across adoption scenarios through 2030. The research validates SkillTree's core thesis: the future of work is not human OR AI, but human-AI skill partnerships.

---

## Key Findings

### The Skill Change Index Methodology

The SCI ranges from 0-100, with higher values indicating greater automation exposure:

| Scenario | Automation Exposure |
|----------|---------------------|
| Midpoint | 25-33% of work hours |
| Faster adoption | Up to 60% for most affected skills |

### Skill Categories by Automation Exposure

**Highest Exposure (Most Change Expected):**
- Digital and information-processing skills
- Routine cognitive work
- SQL programming, detail orientation
- Quality assurance, inventory management
- Invoicing, routine documentation

**Mid-Range Exposure:**
- Communication, customer relations
- Management, writing
- Problem-solving

**Lowest Exposure (Least Change Expected):**
- People-centric skills: leadership, coaching, negotiation
- Empathy, assisting and caring skills
- Multilingualism
- Complex judgment and strategy

## McKinsey's Internal AI Deployment

McKinsey is practicing what it preaches with its "25 Squared" model:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Human employees | 40,000 |
| AI agents deployed | 25,000 (targeting 40,000 by end of 2026) |
| Client-facing roles growth | +25% |
| Back-office roles reduction | -25% |
| Productivity gain | 10% output increase despite 25% fewer back-office staff |
| Hours saved (annual) | 1.5 million |
| AI-generated charts (6 months) | 2.5 million |

---

## Economic Impact

- **$2.9 trillion** potential annual US economic value by 2030
- 70%+ of human skills remain relevant in human-AI collaboration
- "AI fluency" job posting mentions grew **7x in 2 years**
- Developer skills shelf life: **2.5 years** (continuous reskilling required)

---

## Strategic Implications

### Skills Rising in Value
1. AI fluency (using AND orchestrating AI)
2. Human judgment and oversight
3. Complex problem-solving and strategy
4. Client relationship management
5. Quality assurance and verification
6. Teaching and coaching

### Skills Declining in Demand
1. Routine writing and research
2. Basic data processing and synthesis
3. Routine cognitive tasks
4. Document preparation
