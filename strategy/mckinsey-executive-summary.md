## Bottom Line

McKinsey's November 2025 "Skill Change Index" research validates SkillTree's core thesis and presents a **significant market opportunity**. We should integrate SCI data into SkillTree to become the operational platform for enterprise workforce transformation.

---

## Key McKinsey Findings

### The Numbers
- **$2.9 trillion** potential US economic value by 2030 from AI agents and robots
- **25-60%** of work hours exposed to automation (depending on adoption speed)
- **7x growth** in "AI fluency" job posting mentions in just 2 years
- **2.5 years** shelf life for developer skills before requiring updates

### McKinsey Is Practicing What They Preach
McKinsey's own "25 Squared" transformation:
- 40,000 human employees + 25,000 AI agents (targeting 1:1 parity by end of 2026)
- +25% growth in client-facing roles, -25% reduction in back-office
- **10% output increase** despite 25% fewer back-office staff
- 1.5 million hours saved annually through AI-driven search and synthesis

### Skill Categories by Risk

| Risk Level | Skills | Examples |
|------------|--------|----------|
| **High (SCI 50+)** | Digital processing, routine cognitive | SQL programming, invoicing, quality assurance |
| **Medium (SCI 30-50)** | Communication, management, writing | Customer relations, basic content creation |
| **Low (SCI <30)** | People-centric, complex judgment | Leadership, coaching, empathy, negotiation |

## Strategic Opportunity

### The Gap
McKinsey provides research. Enterprises need **operational tools** to:
1. Assess current workforce skills against SCI
2. Plan and execute reskilling investments
3. Manage human-AI skill partnerships
4. Track transition progress

### Our Advantage
SkillTree has the **only unified skill graph** covering both human and AI capabilities:
- 544 skills across 20 branches
- 10-level progression for granular assessment
- Native support for AI agent profiles
- Framework-agnostic skill layer

No competitor (LinkedIn, Workday, LangSmith, MLflow) bridges human + AI skills.
